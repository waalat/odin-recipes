Egusi Soup Recipe

Description

Nigerian Egusi soup is a savoury soup made with ground melon seeds, meats and blended peppers, all fried in palm oil. It is garnished with vegetables and eaten with swallows like the famous fufu.

An important part is to let the egusi cook properly. This reflects in the time allocated above, the time it is added in till it is ready is about 45 mins, this is deliberate. Not cooking well enough could affect the taste and overall outcome of the soup. Pair with your favorite swallow or rice.

Ingredients:

  • ¾ cup egusi seeds or pumpkin seeds
  • 1 ½ pounds cubed beef stew meat
  • salt
  • ½ cup peanut oil
  • 2 large tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 2 habanero peppers, seeded and minced
  • 2 pounds fresh shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 18 ounces tomato sauce
  • 1 ½ cups water
  • 3 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 1 pound fresh spinach, washed and chopped

Steps:

  1. Place the egusi seeds in a blender or food processor and grind until fine.
  2. In a large pot, combine the beef stew meat and salt. Add enough water to cover the meat and bring to a boil. Reduce heat and simmer until the meat is tender, about 1 hour.
  3. In a separate pan, heat the peanut oil over medium heat. Add the chopped tomatoes, onion, and habanero peppers. Cook until the vegetables are soft, about 5 minutes.
  4. Add the ground egusi seeds to the pan and stir well. Cook for another 5 minutes.
  5. Add the cooked beef, shrimp, tomato sauce, water, and tomato paste to the pan. Stir to combine and bring to a simmer.
  6. Reduce the heat to low and let the soup simmer for about 30 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  7. Add the chopped spinach to the soup and cook for an additional 5 minutes, or until the spinach is wilted.
  8. Serve the egusi soup hot with your choice of swallow (fufu, pounded yam, or eba).
