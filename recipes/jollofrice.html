Jollof Rice Recipe

Description

Jollof Rice is a popular West African dish made with rice, tomatoes, onions, and a variety of spices. It is often served at parties and special occasions.

This recipe is for a classic Jollof Rice that is rich in flavor and has a beautiful red color from the tomatoes and peppers. It is typically served with fried plantains and a side of protein such as chicken or fish.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups long-grain parboiled rice
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ginger, grated
  • 1 can (14 ounces) diced tomatoes
  • 1/4 cup tomato paste
  • 2 cups chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1 teaspoon thyme
  • 1 teaspoon curry powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Optional: sliced bell peppers and peas for garnish

Steps:

  1. Heat oil in a large saucepan over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and saut√© until translucent, about 5 minutes.
  2. Add the minced garlic and grated ginger, and cook for another minute until fragrant.
  3. Stir in the diced tomatoes and tomato paste, cooking for about 5 minutes until the mixture thickens.
  4. Add the rice to the saucepan, stirring to coat it with the tomato mixture.
  5. Pour in the chicken or vegetable broth, and add thyme, curry powder, salt, and pepper. Bring to a boil.
  6. Reduce the heat to low, cover the saucepan, and let it simmer for about 20-25 minutes, or until the rice is cooked and has absorbed the liquid.
  7. Fluff the rice with a fork and let it sit covered for an additional 5 minutes.
  8. Optional: Garnish with sliced bell peppers and peas before serving.
